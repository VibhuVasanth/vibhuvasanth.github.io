Jousting - Robotics Competition

Mstcdnj, also known as the Middle School Tech Design Challenge, a competition that took place in Bridgewater Raritan Middle School. There were a total of 5 categories of which I had chosen jousting. The objective was to build a robot to secure a balloon and pop the opponents balloon. The goal was to build a sturdy, compact robot that is able to demolish the opponent robot's balloon. I used a VEX kit to build my robot, I knew my balloon was open to attack, so in order to still follow the regulation and limitation yet still secure my robot I built the balloon in a way that it would move up and down in such a way it will be harder to pinpoint and pop it. I used 2 motors to build this arm, the bigger one being the main for the up and down movement. The other one to help the main motor essentially move up and down. I programmed in a way that when both robots meet in the middle, about 15 meters of the entire track, the arm would ascend to avoid the attack. I incorporated 4 more motors for the wheel to increase the Rpm of the wheels. to move faster, giving me the advantage. I programmed these robots through block coding.

Bucket Ball - Robotics Competition

This year in MSTDCNJ 2026 I chose the Bucketball challenge. A robot has to do certain challenges to gain points such as dropping a ping pong ball into a red cup and taking a jenga block back to its base. This robot used autonomous navigation to get around a 12 by 12. I used a vex kit to build this robot. I used optical, and distance sensors which helped me locate the cup and how far it should be to drop the ball at a precise and perfect angle. I used 4 motors to help my robot stay balanced and steady so it doesn’t miss the cup nor the jenga blocks. I used an algorithm while coding this robot known as the wall following algorithm. This helped me use certain turns and whether or not for it to go straight to make sure all the requirements were satisfied and the obstacles had been avoided. I built a claw using another 2 motors, one being an up and down motion, another that opened and closed the claw to help drop the ball and pick up the cup. I used materials such as the crank arm and 4 by 4 square connectors to help me build this claw. Lastly I programmed another file to help pick up the jenga blocks after the cup had been run and successfully accomplished. I created what's known as a plow system to help drag the jenga blocks along the way until I returned back to base. This robot had won and secured us second place statewide across the region of New Jersey.

Odyssey Of the Mind 2023 - Because iCan

Rube Goldberg chain reaction, whereas you would have to build a series of complex machines to achieve a simple task. That is what we had accomplished in Odyssey of the mind bringing us all the way to the World Finals and winning 5th place. There were a total of 5 problems you could compete and we chose problem #2, all about AI and technology. Our team split into the script writing team and the other being the tech team. I headed the technology side of the team. We had built 4 various machines that had numerous movements. The first machine being a conveyor belt that was built out of pvc pipes and a servo motor. The movement would complete a downward and linear motion. We would roll a ball down the conveyor belt that would trigger the second machine. The ball would land onto a seesaw that would push the ball to the other side. The ball would roll down an intricate maze and eventually fall into a glass held by a string. When the weight of the ball forces the glass to fall down it would trigger the motion of a pulley system. When the glass falls down the string would on the pulley system to uncover a key. When the key is uncovered a string is attached to the box to help jumpstart the 3rd machine. Where the flap is pulled out of the way, for a tennis ball to roll down and hit another flap. This is the circular motion. After that, the string would begin to unroll to pull a pen. When the pen is pulled that is the trigger of the 4th and final machine. A ping pong ball would roll down, hit a flap that opened a book. These are the 4 machines that I built with my team and took leadership in this specific part.

Odyssey of the Mind 2024 - AI Tech No Art

For the following year in Odyssey of the mind, I had built a Robotic Arm. This project also made us a top 20 world finalist. We built a robotic arm that is integrated with AI to build a beautiful piece of art. We used components from Arduino to build this arm. We used led lights to signal whether or not it was a great piece of art. If it had turned green that must mean it was beautiful, yet if it turned red it meant it was a horrible piece of art. We used multiple servo motors to increase in motion ability. 3 for the up and down movement of the arm and fingers. 2 servo motors to help it turn left to right. THe motion would turn a full 360 degrees and 180 degrees up and down. There would be a button stuck to the breadboard that would help initiate this task. We installed Arduino IDE and coded this robot in the language C++.
